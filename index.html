How do the crystals work? ▼
The Charge
Each crystal has an energy that slowly fades when you're alone. It drains fast at first, then slower and slower — but it never fully dies. Even after days alone, your crystal will still produce a faint flicker every 30 seconds or so. Barely alive. Waiting.

Charging Up
When you're near your friends' crystals, they charge each other. The more friends nearby, the faster the charge. The closer they are, the brighter the glow. A few minutes together and your crystal is back to full strength.

The Effects

Alone (charged): Gentle heartbeat pulse in your color. Slowly dims and slows over hours as the charge fades.

Alone (low charge): Faint, infrequent flicker. The color washes out and becomes muted. Your crystal is lonely.

1 friend nearby: Your crystal wakes up. Their color starts slowly bleeding into your pulse — a smooth fade between your color and theirs.

More friends: Your crystal cycles through everyone's colors with smooth transitions. The closer a friend is, the stronger their color appears. Brighter, more alive.

Everyone together: All crystals glow bright, rotating through every color in the group, pulsing in perfect sync. It takes about 30 seconds for crystals to sync up — a new arrival slowly drifts into rhythm with the group.

Goodbyes
When a friend leaves, their color fades out of your crystal over about 5 minutes — like a lingering memory.

When a friend arrives after you've been alone for a while, your crystal does a quick bright flash — gasping awake.

Groups
Crystals with the same group number interact with each other. Different groups are invisible to each other — like they're on different frequencies.
